**2. What explains the erosion of the cash balance?**

Looking at our current assets since 2012, you can see a troubling divergence. Our inventory is climbing rapidly as we expand our plant varieties and shift to mature plants with 2-5 year growing cycles. Meanwhile, our cash balance is steadily declining. We are asset-rich, but cash-poor because our capital is literally buried in the ground.


Our revenue is growing beautifully, crossing the $1 million mark. However, we are operating under a 'Growth Paradox.' Because we are entirely self-funded and completely averse to debt, every dollar of this expansion is coming out of our cash reserves. Furthermore, we are aggressively paying our suppliers within 10 days to capture a 2% discount. While this saves pennies on margins, it is severely draining our immediate liquidity.

If we compare our working capital efficiency to industry benchmarks, the root of our cash flow problem becomes obvious. We are holding inventory for almost 100 days longer than the industry average. It takes us almost 51 days to collect from customers, compared to the 22-day norm. Yet, we are paying our suppliers in under 10 days, while the industry standard is 27 days. We are paying out immediately, but waiting far too long to get paid back.
